Homicide

Homicide unit detectives investigate murders by assessing the scene, collecting evidence, dusting fingerprints, photographing, and interviewing witnesses.

Cybercrimes

Many of the crimes that occur in the real world can also, unfortunately, take place online. These cybercrimes include identity theft, illegal firearms sales, online drug trafficking and online harassment.

Forensic investigations

Many different types of crimes are investigated using forensics. The police detectives in this special unit scientifically analyze evidence from crime scenes to piece together the truth about what happened and who committed the crime.

Fraud

raud occurs when someone intentionally uses false information to scam a person or company, usually for financial gain.

Narcotics

Narcotics detectives are responsible for investigating drug-related crimes. This includes smuggling, sales, the growing or production of drugs, and even the use of fraud to obtain prescription drugs illegally.

Gang violence

These detectives focus on ending violence between gangs in a particular city. Some of their investigations may overlap with narcotics cases, while others involve investigating offenders in gang-related crimes

frequantly Asked a Question

What is criminal investigation?

Criminal investigation refers to the process of gathering evidence, examining facts, and uncovering the truth in criminal cases.

What are the main objectives of a criminal investigation?

The main objectives of a criminal investigation are to determine whether a crime has been committed, identify the perpetrator, gather evidence, and support the prosecution of the case.

What are some common methods used in criminal investigations?

Common methods used in criminal investigations include interviewing witnesses and suspects, collecting and analyzing physical evidence, conducting surveillance, and utilizing forensic techniques.

What is the importance of preserving a crime scene?

Preserving a crime scene is crucial to ensure that evidence remains intact and uncontaminated. It allows investigators to reconstruct events accurately and gather valuable evidence.

What is the difference between direct and circumstantial evidence?

Direct evidence directly proves a fact, such as eyewitness testimony or surveillance footage. Circumstantial evidence, on the other hand, indirectly implies a fact based on other circumstances or evidence.

What is the Miranda warning, and why is it important in criminal investigations?

The Miranda warning is a statement read to individuals in custody, informing them of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. It is important to ensure that individuals are aware of their rights during the investigation process.

What is the role of collaboration in a criminal investigation?

Collaboration between law enforcement agencies, forensic experts, prosecutors, and other stakeholders is essential in a criminal investigation. It allows for the sharing of resources, expertise, and information, increasing the chances of a successful investigation and prosecution.
